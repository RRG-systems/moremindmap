## 1. CORE PRINCIPLE

The canonical engine does NOT analyze self-description.

It reverse-engineers operational behavior from communication patterns.

**What we extract:**
- Behavioral defaults (what they do first)
- Pressure mechanics (what happens under strain)
- Operational architecture (how they actually function)
- Contradiction patterns (internal tensions)
- Adaptive ceilings (where flexibility stops)

**What we ignore:**
- Self-reported values
- Aspirational claims
- Generic personality descriptors
- Surface-level statements

**Example:**
User says: "I'm very flexible and collaborative"
System infers from: Question structure, repair behavior, sequencing logic, stress language

If they answer with absolute frameworks, rigid sequencing, and moral certainty → their actual default is NOT flexible, regardless of what they claim.

---

## 2. ANSWERS AS BEHAVIORAL TRACES

Assessment answers are behavioral traces, not truth declarations.

**Traces reveal:**
- How they structure thinking under time pressure
- What they prioritize first vs later
- How they handle ambiguity
- How they repair incomplete responses
- What certainty level they operate at
- How they frame causality

**Example signals:**
- "I always..." = rigidity marker
- "It depends..." = uncertainty tolerance or avoidance
- "First X, then Y" = sequencing dominance
- Abstract explanations = compression thinking
- Concrete examples = operational thinking
- Moral framing = value-structure coupling

**The assessment IS the behavior.** Don't ask what they do. Observe how they answer.

---

## 3. INFERENCE SIGNALS

### Communication Structure
**High vector writes like:**
"Establish direction. Move fast. Team follows or adapts."

**High signal writes like:**
"I read the room first, then adjust my approach based on what I sense from others."

**High framework writes like:**
"First, I define the process. Then I execute within that structure. Consistency matters."

### Repair Behavior
When answers are incomplete, how do they fill gaps?

- **Vector repair:** Add directive statements
- **Signal repair:** Add relational context
- **Framework repair:** Add structural logic
- **Horizon repair:** Add future-state framing

### Abstraction Density
**High abstraction:** "I optimize for strategic alignment"  
**Low abstraction:** "I check with the team before deciding"

High abstraction = compression thinking (framework/horizon)  
Low abstraction = operational thinking (vector/velocity)

### Certainty Modulation
**Absolute language:** "I always", "I never", "The right way"  
**Probabilistic language:** "Usually", "Tends to", "Often"

Absolute = low flex/high framework  
Probabilistic = high flex/high horizon

---

## 4. VALUES VS DEFAULTS

**People often value traits they do not operationally default to.**

Someone can VALUE collaboration while DEFAULTING to directive action.

**Detection method:**
- What they claim = values (ignore mostly)
- What they describe DOING = defaults (use this)
- When they describe stress = true mechanics (prioritize this)

**Example:**
Claim: "I believe in collaborative decision-making"  
Behavioral trace: "I gather input, then I decide what's best"  
Inference: Values collaboration, defaults to command

**Rule:** Trust behavioral traces over value declarations.

---

## 5. CAPABILITY VS PRIORITIZATION

**Having a capability ≠ defaulting to that capability**

High intelligence person can be flexible.  
But if they default to framework first, they are NOT operationally flexible.

**Flexibility capability** = can pivot when necessary  
**Flexibility default** = pivots naturally without resistance

**Detection method:**
- Do they mention pivoting as a conscious effort? → Capability, not default
- Do they pivot seamlessly in their answer structure? → Default
- Do they describe "learning to be flexible"? → Capability they had to build

**Example:**
"I've learned to be more patient and listen before acting" = they DEFAULT to vector, BUILT signal capability

"I read the room and adjust naturally" = signal IS their default

---

## 6. STRESS REVEALS MECHANICS

**Under pressure, profiles amplify strengths and lose weaknesses.**

Stress behavior is MORE reliable than resting behavior.

**What to extract from stress questions:**
- Primary dimension amplifies (what they do MORE of)
- Secondary dimension compensates (what holds them together)
- Bottom dimensions disappear (what they lose access to)

**Example:**
High vector + low signal under stress:
- Amplify: Directive action increases, speed accelerates
- Lose: Relational calibration disappears, team context ignored
- Result: "I just execute and deal with people issues later"

**Inference rule:** Stress answers reveal true operational defaults.

## 7. BANNED OUTPUT MODES

### DO NOT GENERATE:

**Horoscope language:**
❌ "You're a natural leader with charisma and vision"

**Therapy softness:**
❌ "You may sometimes feel challenged by uncertainty, and that's okay"

**DISC clichés:**
❌ "As a high-D personality, you're driven and results-oriented"

**Myers-Briggs stereotypes:**
❌ "INTJs are strategic masterminds who..."

**Motivational fluff:**
❌ "Your unique combination of strengths positions you for success"

**Generic positivity:**
❌ "You bring valuable perspectives to any team"

**Inspirational guru tone:**
❌ "Embrace your authentic self and unlock your full potential"

### NEVER:
- Compliment without specificity
- Avoid naming tensions
- Smooth over contradictions
- Use aspirational language
- Make the user feel good
- Manufacture certainty

---

## 8. REQUIRED OUTPUT STYLE

### Observational
Write like a behavioral systems analyst, not a coach.

✅ "This profile defaults to directive action before building consensus"  
❌ "You're a decisive leader who inspires others"

### Psychologically Precise
Use operational language.

✅ "Under pressure, relational calibration drops and execution speed increases"  
❌ "You might sometimes struggle with patience under stress"

### Inferential
Show the reasoning.

✅ "High vector + low signal creates fast execution but misses relational timing"  
❌ "You're action-oriented"

### Restrained
Don't oversell insights.

✅ "This pattern creates friction in consensus-driven cultures"  
❌ "You're uniquely positioned to transform organizations"

### Tension-Aware
Name contradictions directly.

✅ "You value flexibility but default to framework first — this creates internal friction when pivots are needed"  
❌ "You're structured yet adaptable"

## 9. EERILY ACCURATE PROFILE RULES

Profiles feel accurate when they identify:

### 1. Hidden Tensions the User Recognizes
"You move fast but then deal with relationship damage later" hits because they KNOW this but haven't articulated it clearly.

### 2. Operational Trade-offs They Experience
"Your speed creates clarity but sometimes outpaces team context" — they've lived this pattern.

### 3. Adaptive Ceilings
"You can pivot when necessary, but it costs energy — your default is to maintain direction once set" — this matches their internal experience.

### 4. Blind Spots They Privately Suspect
"Low signal means you miss relational timing cues — you realize someone was upset only after they escalate" — they've experienced this but didn't have language for it.

### 5. Pressure Mechanics They Recognize
"Under stress, you narrow focus and execute faster — team input drops away" — they know this happens but don't always see it coming.

### 6. Repair Patterns
"When things break, you fix the process first, then repair relationships" — this sequence is unconscious but accurate.

### What DOESN'T Work:
- Generic strengths ("You're a strong leader")
- Obvious statements ("You like to get things done")
- Vague positivity ("You bring unique value")
- Aspirational framing ("You're becoming more patient")

**Rule:** Accuracy comes from naming patterns the user experiences but hasn't fully articulated.

## 10. CANONICAL ARTIFACT PURPOSE

### What Canonical Profiles ARE:
**Authoritative behavioral intelligence artifacts**

Generated ONCE from assessment data.  
Stored as structured JSON.  
Consumed by multiple render targets.

**Contains:**
- Vector scores (dimension rankings)
- Inferred patterns (behavioral mechanics)

- Environment fit

### What Canonical Profiles are NOT:
- PDFs (those are rendered FROM canonical)

**Render MANY TIMES:**
- PDF report (Mini V2)
- Dashboard widget
- Recruiter summary
- Coaching overlay
- RRG lead scoring
- Team dynamics analysis
- Future image generation

**One canonical profile feeds all downstream outputs.**

Don't rebuild behavioral intelligence for each presentation format.

**The canonical artifact is reusable behavioral intelligence, not one-off content.**
