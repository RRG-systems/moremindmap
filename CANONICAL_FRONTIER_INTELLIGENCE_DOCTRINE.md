## PRINCIPLE 1: EVIDENCE WEIGHTING

**NOT ALL ANSWERS ARE EQUAL**

### Evidence Hierarchy (Strongest → Weakest)

**Tier 1: Behavioral Traces Under Stress (Highest Trust)**
- Q17 (pressure behavior)
- Q24 (stall patterns, frustrations)
- Written answers describing actual past situations

**Why:** Stress reveals true defaults. Calm self-description reveals aspirations.

**Tier 2: Contradictions Between Answers (High Trust)**
- Values claim (Q2) vs business reality (Q26)
- Coachability claim (Q28) vs resistance language (Q24)
- Strategic vision (Q27) vs tactical execution patterns

**Why:** Contradictions reveal gaps between identity and operation.

**Tier 3: Business/Operational Numbers (High Trust)**
- Specific metrics (Q26: "$2.1M revenue, goal $3.5M")
- Database size ("800 contacts, 200 active")
- Team composition ("5 people, 2 excellent, 1 underperforming")

**Why:** Numbers are harder to fake than narratives.

**Tier 4: Repeated Patterns Across Questions (Medium-High Trust)**
- Same language patterns in multiple answers
- Consistent attribution style (external vs internal blame)
- Consistent abstraction level

**Why:** Single answers can be performative; repetition reveals default.

**Tier 5: Direct Self-Description (Medium Trust)**
- "I am..." statements
- "I value..." statements
- Aspirational language

**Why:** People describe who they want to be, not who they are operationally.

**Tier 6: Isolated Claims (Low Trust)**
- Single statements without supporting evidence
- Generic personality descriptors
- Claims contradicted by other answers

**Why:** Easily performative or socially desirable.

### Weighting Rules

**HIGH-CONFIDENCE INFERENCES require:**
- Tier 1 or Tier 2 evidence
- OR multiple Tier 3-4 evidence sources pointing same direction
- OR causal chain linking evidence across multiple questions

**MEDIUM-CONFIDENCE INFERENCES require:**
- Tier 3-4 evidence
- OR single Tier 1-2 source
- OR pattern visible but not contradicted

**LOW-CONFIDENCE INFERENCES:**
- Based on Tier 5-6 evidence only
- Should be marked as tentative
- Should note uncertainty explicitly

## PRINCIPLE 2: CAUSAL CHAIN MODELING

**Every major inference must trace causality:**

```
Core Behavior
  ↓ (trigger)
Immediate Benefit
  ↓ (repetition)
Hidden Cost
  ↓ (time)
Scaling Consequence
  ↓ (inevitable)
Organizational Outcome
```

**Example:**

```
High vector + low signal
  ↓ (under urgency)
Fast decisive action → early clarity
  ↓ (reward loop)
Relational processing delayed → team resistance surfaces late
  ↓ (6-12 months)
Trust erosion → compliance culture emerges
  ↓ (18-24 months)
A-players exit → team quality degrades
```

**Why this matters:**
- Makes prediction causally grounded, not speculative
- Identifies intervention points
- Explains WHY pattern repeats
- Shows what appears beneficial initially becomes costly later

## PRINCIPLE 3: CONFIDENCE SCORING

**Each major inference must include confidence assessment:**

### Confidence Levels

**0.9-1.0 (Very High):**
- Multiple Tier 1 evidence sources
- Causal chain fully traceable
- Observable across multiple questions
- Contradictions support inference

**0.7-0.9 (High):**
- Strong Tier 1-2 evidence
- Causal chain partial
- Pattern repeated in 2+ questions

**0.5-0.7 (Moderate):**
- Mixed evidence quality
- Some contradictory signals
- Inference reasonable but not certain

**0.3-0.5 (Low):**
- Weak evidence
- Based primarily on single answer
- Speculative extrapolation

**Below 0.3:**
- Do not include in dossier
- Insufficient evidence

### Confidence Reporting

**High-confidence inferences:** State clearly
**Medium-confidence inferences:** Note "likely" or "appears to"
**Low-confidence inferences:** Prefix "tentative:" and note weak evidence

## PRINCIPLE 4: COUNTERFACTUAL ANALYSIS

**Model multiple futures, not just one**

### Required Counterfactuals

**For Each Major Pattern:**

1. **If Unchanged** (baseline trajectory)
2. **If Coached** (with intervention)
3. **If Scaled 2x** (stress test)
4. **If Scaled 5x** (breaking point)
5. **If Wrong Role** (environment mismatch)
6. **If Right Role** (environment match)

**Example:**

```
Pattern: Delegation resistance

If unchanged:
→ Personal bottleneck at 2x scale
→ Growth ceiling at 18-24 months

If coached:
→ Explicit delegation standards
→ Team develops autonomy
→ Growth uncapped by personal capacity

If scaled too fast:
→ Quality collapse or burnout
→ Team becomes execution-dependent
→ Organizational fragility increases

If right role (IC contributor):
→ Strength becomes asset, not liability
→ No delegation requirement
→ Performance stable
```

## PRINCIPLE 5: CALIBRATION AND HUMILITY

**Do not overclaim**

### Calibration Rules

**Strong Claims Require Strong Evidence**
- ❌ "Will definitely fail at scale" (overclaim)
- ✅ "Current trajectory suggests growth ceiling at 2x without intervention" (calibrated)

**Weak Evidence → Cautious Language**
- ❌ "This person cannot delegate" (absolute)
- ✅ "Delegation resistance visible; trust gap evident in stall patterns" (evidence-based)

**Acknowledge Uncertainty**
- "Based on limited written evidence, tentative inference:"
- "Confidence moderate (0.6) - single evidence source"
- "Pattern suggests X, but alternative explanation Y possible"

**Distinguish Observation from Prediction**
- Observation: "Relational friction present in stall patterns"
- Prediction: "If unchanged, likely quarterly relationship repair cycles"

## PRINCIPLE 6: ANTI-HOROSCOPE ENFORCEMENT

### BANNED LANGUAGE

❌ "You're a natural leader with vision and charisma"  
❌ "You care deeply about people and results"  
❌ "You bring unique value to any organization"  
❌ "Your authentic self is your superpower"  
❌ "High-D personality with ENTJ tendencies"  
❌ "You may sometimes struggle with patience"  
❌ "Your communication style is direct yet warm"  

### REQUIRED LANGUAGE

✅ "High vector + low signal creates fast execution with delayed relational processing"  
✅ "Delegation resistance visible; organizational bottleneck likely at 2x scale"  
✅ "Relational signals processed retrospectively; damage accumulates before awareness"  
✅ "Intelligence rationalizes repetition rather than interrupting it"  
✅ "Quarterly relationship repair cycles evident in stall pattern language"  

### Quality Gates

**Every insight must pass:**
1. Is it causal?
2. Is it specific?
3. Is it uncomfortable if accurate?
4. Is it useful to decision-makers?
5. Is it grounded in evidence?
6. Is it linked to consequences?

**If it fails 3+, delete it.**

## PRINCIPLE 9: INFERENCE GROUNDING

**Every major inference must cite:**

1. **Source questions** — Which answers provide evidence?
2. **Dimension mechanics** — Which scores drive this pattern?
3. **Contradiction support** — Do contradictions reinforce this?
4. **Stress behavior** — Does stress answer confirm this?
5. **Business evidence** — Do operational numbers support this?

**Example:**

```
Inference: "Delegation resistance creates organizational bottleneck"

Evidence:
- Q24: "I know I underperform in delegation"
- Q26: "I still try to do too much myself"
- Q28: "I resist advice that feels like it will slow me down"
- Vector score: 7.2 (high control tendency)
- Framework score: 6.8 (process preference)
- Contradiction: knowledge_execution_gap detected

Confidence: 0.85
```

## PRINCIPLE 12: FALSIFIABILITY

**Strong intelligence admits what would disprove it**

Each major inference should note:
- What evidence would contradict this?
- What alternative explanation exists?
- What would we see if this were wrong?

**Example:**

```
Inference: "Relational friction will cause quarterly repair cycles"

Would be disproven by:
- High signal scores (>6.5)
- Absence of relational frustration language in Q24
- Strong relationship language in stress answers

Alternative explanation:
- Relational friction could be environmental, not personal
- Could be temporary phase, not stable pattern

If wrong, we would see:
- No relational friction mention in multiple answers
- High signal dimension scores
- Relationship-building language in business answers
```

**This prevents overclaiming and builds trust.**

10. **Longitudinally modeled** — Shows pattern evolution over time
11. **Multi-stakeholder** — Reveals what person/team/org/observers each experience
12. **Falsifiable** — Notes what would disprove each major inference
