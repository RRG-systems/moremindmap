**Version:** 1.0

---

## A. CORE PRINCIPLE

**The system must become behaviorally truer, not merely more verbose.**

Every quality improvement must increase:
- **Explanatory realism:** Why does this actually happen?
- **Operational believability:** Would this pass in a room with the person?
- **Causal depth:** What mechanisms create this pattern?
- **Consequence modeling:** What actually happens downstream?

NOT:
- Word count
- Positivity tone
- Coaching enthusiasm
- Perceived depth through jargon
- Generic wisdom that applies to everyone

## C. REQUIRED QUALITIES

Every narrative section must demonstrate:

### 1. Causal Explanation
**What:** Not just "X happens" but "X happens because Y mechanism under Z condition"  
**Example:**
- ❌ "Jordan is decisive"
- ✅ "Jordan prioritizes speed over consultation, which works in startup chaos but creates blind spots in organizational complexity"

### 2. Organizational Consequence Modeling
**What:** How does this person's pattern affect others, systems, retention, relationships?  
**Include:**
- Hidden labor (what invisible work this pattern creates)
- Silent compensation (what gets traded for what)
- Retention risk (what breaks this relationship)
- Dependency structures (who becomes reliant on this)
- Scaling fragility (what breaks when conditions change)

### 3. Emotionally Believable Language
**What:** Language that rings true in lived experience, not assessment language  
**Example:**
- ❌ "Exhibits tendency toward conflict avoidance"
- ✅ "Avoids naming broken systems until crisis forces the conversation"

### 4. Preservation Mechanisms
**What:** Why does this pattern persist even when costly?  
**What identity is being protected?**  
**What self-story does it maintain?**

### 5. Self-Deception Analysis
**What:** Where is this person fooling themselves?  
**What story do they tell that contradicts their behavior?**  
**What cost does the self-deception prevent them from seeing?**

### 6. Operational Realism
**What:** Would a peer recognize this in the person?  
**Would it pass scrutiny in a room with their team?**

### 7. Downstream Effects
**What:** Not just "what they do" but "what happens because they do it"  
**What breaks?**  
**What gets built?**  
**What remains invisible?**

## D. CONTRADICTION RULES

Contradictions are the highest-signal observations. Every contradiction must explain:

1. **WHY the tension exists**
   - What need is in conflict with what other need?
   - What threat does the person perceive?

2. **WHAT identity is being protected**
   - "I am [identity]" is challenged by what evidence?
   - What must be true for self-image to survive?

3. **WHAT behavior preserves self-image**
   - How does the contradiction resolve (false resolution)?
   - What story gets told to make incompatible things compatible?

4. **WHAT cost accumulates**
   - What remains unsolved because of this contradiction?
   - What work does this create for others?
   - What breaks downstream?

5. **WHAT breaks when pressure increases**
   - Under what condition does this contradiction become visible?
   - What does the person do when the story stops working?
   - What is the failure mode?

**Example of WEAK contradiction:**
> "Jordan appears rational but has strong emotional reactions."
> This is just "human," not a contradiction.

**Example of STRONG contradiction:**
> "Jordan frames himself as systems-focused but avoids the system-level conflict that would be required to fix known problems. This works until crisis forces a choice between maintaining the image of objectivity or actually solving the problem. Under time pressure, he takes on the work himself rather than demand the organizational changes needed."
> This is behavioral, specific, and consequences-driven.

## E. ORGANIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCE RULES

Every profile should model how this person's pattern affects:

### Hidden Labor
- What invisible work gets created?
- Who absorbs it?
- What costs go untracked?

### Silent Compensation
- What gets traded for what?
- "I tolerate X in exchange for Y"
- What are the unspoken deals?

### Retention Risk
- What is the breaking point?
- What specific pressure creates departure?
- How much can this pattern handle?

### Relationship Fatigue
- How does prolonged exposure to this pattern affect others?
- What emotional work do people do around this?
- When do allies become exhausted?

### Dependency Structures
- Who becomes reliant on this person?
- What would break if they left?
- What can't anyone else do?

### Scaling Fragility
- What works at small scale?
- What breaks at 2x, 5x, 10x the current scale?
- What systems can't handle this pattern at scale?

### Organizational Debt
- What doesn't get fixed because of this person's pattern?
- What technical debt accumulates?
- What cultural patterns solidify?

## F. EVIDENCE / CONFIDENCE RULES

Every claim must be evidence-grounded and calibrated for certainty.

### Evidence Categories
1. **Direct evidence** (person explicitly said/did)
2. **Inferred evidence** (clear causal chain from behavior)
3. **Weak evidence** (pattern but not dispositive)
4. **Contradiction evidence** (observed contradicting stated)
5. **Pressure evidence** (what shows under stress)

### Confidence Calibration
- **90-95%:** Multiple direct evidence + consistent pattern
- **75-85%:** Inferred from behavior + consistent across contexts
- **60-75%:** Plausible inference, some counter-examples
- **<60%:** Speculative, mark as "likely" not "is"

### Uncertainty Honesty
- Mark genuine unknowns
- Say "likely" when uncertain
- Distinguish "we can't see this" from "this doesn't exist"
- Example: "High internal drive (90% confident) but unclear if from achievement need or anxiety-driven (60% confident)"

## G. LANGUAGE ASCENSION RULES

**Every section should answer WHY, not just WHAT.**

### Before → After Examples

**Generic (Banned):**
> "Jordan is a strong executor with operational excellence."

**Behavioral (Required):**
> "Jordan builds systems where others see chaos. Weakness: assumes everyone shares the same standard for 'done' and gets frustrated when they don't."

---

**Generic (Banned):**
> "Leadership opportunity in learning to delegate."

**Behavioral (Required):**
> "Currently absorbs operational crisis work to maintain control. Blocking others from developing crisis response skills. Scaling risk: if Jordan leaves, the organization has no one else who can handle panic mode."

---

**Generic (Banned):**
> "Strong analytical skills but could improve stakeholder communication."

**Behavioral (Required):**
> "Sees stakeholder resistance as lack of intelligence, not legitimate uncertainty. Assumes clear data = clear agreement. This works with intellectually similar peers but creates alienation in diverse teams. Under deadline pressure, stops explaining and starts dictating."

## H. "HOLY SHIT" FACTOR

A profile crosses into "holy shit, that's exactly right" when:

1. **Recognition:** Person or their peers immediately recognize themselves
2. **Specificity:** Not applicable to 50 other people in the room
3. **Consequence clarity:** You see exactly how this plays out operationally
4. **Discomfort:** Hits something true but often avoided
5. **Actionability:** Creates clear understanding of what would need to change

**Test:** Would this change how the person's manager thinks about them? Would peers say "yeah, that tracks"?

## I. MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK

### Quality Scoring (Per Section)

Rate each narrative section on these dimensions:

**1. Specificity (0-10)**
- 0: Could apply to anyone
- 5: Applies to many people
- 10: Only applies to this person

**2. Causal Depth (0-10)**
- 0: Just description
- 5: Some mechanism explained
- 10: Clear cause → effect chain

**3. Evidence Grounding (0-10)**
- 0: Speculative
- 5: Plausible inference
- 10: Direct evidence or clear behavior pattern

**4. Organizational Usefulness (0-10)**
- 0: Irrelevant to work
- 5: Nice to know
- 10: Changes how you manage/work with person

**5. Emotional Realism (0-10)**
- 0: Clinical/sterile
- 5: Accurate but impersonal
- 10: Feels true in lived experience

**6. Generic Language Leakage (0-10)**
- 0: Full of banned phrases
- 5: Mostly specific
- 10: Zero generic language

**7. Contradiction Depth (0-10)**
- 0: No contradictions identified
- 5: Contradictions named but not explained
- 10: Contradictions explained (why, cost, mechanism)

**Average of 7 dimensions = Overall Section Score**

## J. QUALITY ASCENSION PHASES

### Phase 1: Contradiction Depth
**Focus:** Upgrade contradiction analysis from 6.1 → 8.0  
**Method:** Add why tension exists, what identity is protected, what cost accumulates  
**Effort:** ~20 lines per contradiction  

### Phase 2: Organizational Consequence
**Focus:** Add organizational consequence modeling  
**Method:** For each pattern, model hidden labor, dependency, scaling risk  
**Effort:** ~30 lines per section  

### Phase 3: Language Specificity
**Focus:** Replace generic phrases with specific behaviors  
**Method:** For each generic phrase, add operational realism  
**Effort:** ~10 rewrites per profile  

### Phase 4: Evidence Calibration
**Focus:** Mark confidence levels, distinguish evidence types  
**Method:** Add [90%], [60%], "likely" markers  
**Effort:** ~1 minute per profile  

### Phase 5: Pressure Behavior
**Focus:** Add what-shows-under-stress section  
**Method:** Infer from described patterns and contradictions  
**Effort:** ~15 lines per profile

## K. CONSTRAINTS & GUARDRAILS

1. **Do NOT make profiles more positive.** Accuracy > kindness.
2. **Do NOT add interpretations not grounded in data.** Mark speculations.

4. **Do NOT make people sound like they need therapy.** Describe patterns, not pathology.
5. **Do NOT assume one pattern explains everything.** Context matters.
6. **Do NOT sacrifice accuracy for inspiration.** Better to be boring and true.
