## Confidence Calibration Doctrine

**How confident should the profile be?**

```
95% confidence: Multiple direct answers from assessment
  Example: "You stated 5 times you avoid conflict directly"

85% confidence: Pattern across answers, clear behavioral trace
  Example: "5 different answers point to contradiction between stated values and behavior"

75% confidence: Inference from related patterns, some confirmation bias risk
  Example: "Your pressure response suggests X, inferred from stated decision speed + leadership examples"

70% confidence: Inferred from limited data, could be wrong
  Example: "Hidden costs inferred from stated org context; haven't seen actual damage"

50% confidence: Speculation, explicitly labeled
  Example: "If this pattern continues X years, likely consequence: [speculative]"
```

## Causal Realism Doctrine

**What's a real mechanism vs bullshit?**

### Real Mechanism (Testable)

> "Sees problems faster than team can respond. Creates expectation that you'll solve them. Role expands. Constraints increase. Freedom decreases. Response: work harder. Consequence: unsustainability."

✅ Checkable: Does team expect your solutions? Are constraints increasing? Is workload unsustainable?

### Bullshit (Untestable)

> "Natural leader with authentic presence and innate ability to inspire."

❌ Unfalsifiable: What's "authentic presence"? Why "inspire"? No mechanism.

### Real Mechanism (Behavioral)

> "Directness creates clarity. Clarity feels like authority. Authority attracts decisions toward you. You become bottleneck. Bottleneck creates delays. Delays create frustration. You delegate to relieve bottleneck. Delegated person fails because they lacked context. Context was yours alone. Cycle repeats."

✅ Checkable: Do decisions accumulate on you? Do delegations fail? Why?

## Contradiction Doctrine

**When is a contradiction REAL vs FAKE?**

### Real Contradiction (Holds Person)

> "Values flexibility AND consistency. Values emerge from different contexts:
> - Flexibility: 'I adapt to what works' (learning context)
> - Consistency: 'I maintain principles' (integrity context)
> These don't coexist. Under pressure, one dominates. Which? Depends on pressure source:
>   - Moral pressure → consistency wins, flexibility buried
>   - Execution pressure → consistency sacrificed for speed
> Person doesn't see this switch. Feels consistent throughout."

✅ Unfolds behavior: Why does pressure type determine response? What does person miss?

### Fake Contradiction (Therapy Session)

> "Wants autonomy but fears independence."

❌ Vague, could mean anything, reads like pop psychology

## Overfitting Risks (What NOT To Do)

❌ **Over-Precision:** Claiming 95% confidence on inferred data
- Too specific feels false
- Better: "Pattern suggests X; confidence 75% based on limited answers"

❌ **Psychological Depth Overreach:** Using therapy/coaching language
- Sounds authoritative but rings false
- Better: Behavioral mechanism + observable cost

❌ **Pathologizing Normal:** Framing personality as disorder
- "May struggle with perfectionism" ← Reads like DSM-5 cosplay
- Better: "Perfectionism-as-leverage: ensures quality, costs speed"

❌ **Future Prediction:** Stating trajectory as fact
- "Will burnout in 3 years" ← Unfalsifiable, sounds desperate
- Better: "Current trajectory unsustainable at scale; breaking point likely 2-3 years without change"

❌ **Moral Judgment:** Framing patterns as good/bad
- "Is thoughtful and strategic" ← Positive gloss
- Better: "Systems thinking creates advantage AND blindness to social cost"
